Instructions

  1. Make the Patties

    • In a bowl, combine ground beef, salt, pepper, and optional ingredients (onion, garlic powder, Worcestershire).
    • Divide into 4 equal parts and shape into patties (slightly wider than the buns since they shrink when cooking).
    • Press a small dent in the center of each patty with your thumb (prevents puffing up).

  2. Prepare the Sauce

    • Mix all burger sauce ingredients in a bowl. Chill until needed.

  3. Cook the Patties

    • Heat a skillet or grill over medium-high heat.
    • Cook patties for about 3 to 4 minutes per side (or until your desired doneness).
    • Add cheese during the last minute if using, and cover to melt.

  4. Toast the Buns

    • Cut buns in half and lightly butter them.
    • Toast on a pan or grill until golden brown.

  5. Assemble the Burger

    • Spread sauce on both bun halves.
    • Add lettuce, tomato, and pickles to the bottom bun.
    • Place the hot patty with melted cheese.
    • Add onions (raw or grilled) if you like.
    • Top with the top bun.

TIPS

  • Do not overmix the meat — it makes patties tough.
  • Use fresh, soft buns (brioche or sesame).
  • Want it spicy? Add chili flakes or jalapeños.
  • Prefer chicken? Use ground chicken or a grilled breast instead.
